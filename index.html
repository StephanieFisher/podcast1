Podcast Review 1

Homecoming

Structure

First, the narrator introduces the show, then we jump right in with an interview between the main character and a returning vet. There is a lot of jumping around, plot wise, and I found it hard to follow at first, but was able to figure it out via contex clues.

Who does the talking?

The characters; there is no narrator.

Is it scripted?

Yes.

Interviews?

The story of the podcast seems to be based around interviews--and not just ones between the main character and the returning military people.

Are there sound effects and music?

There are a bunch of sound effects from background objects and locations that seem to be trying to immerse the viewer in the show. Not much music, though.

Radiolab

Structure

This podcast is set up like a series of informal lectures and interviews with experts in their fields.

Who is talking?

The hosts first introduce the topic, then the guest, then the guest does most of the talking.

Is it scripted?

The outline of it seems to be scripted, but what the guests say isn't, as the hosts seem surprized. But they could be acting.

Interviews?

This show seems to be based around interviews with experts in the field, as each episode has someone new come in and talk about what they know.

Are there sound effects and music?

Yes. They used: clips from other lectures, scifi sounds, a choir, and other songs
